About

I'm a chemical engineering student at NTHU in Taiwan, interested in energy, consumer products, and how science becomes something people genuinely use and care about.

Since my teens, I've intentionally placed myself in unfamiliar environments — from working at a hostel in London to studying abroad at Case Western Reserve University and solo traveling through Guatemala.

While engineering trained me to think in systems, I've become equally fascinated by the human side of products: why certain technologies succeed, how brands shape behavior, and how chemistry quietly influences everyday life — from industrial processes to skincare and beauty formulations.

Lately, much of my curiosity revolves around climate technology, manufacturing, consumer chemistry, and entrepreneurship.

This site is where I document what I'm learning through projects, conversations, travel, and experimentation as I figure out what kinds of things are worth building.
